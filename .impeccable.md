## Design Context

### Users
Students and professors viewing a PICT course project at KTH. Primary audience is other student teams and course staff evaluating the project. Secondary audience is anyone who finds the public GitHub Pages URL. Context: desktop browsing during work hours, evaluating a mobile app concept.

### Brand Personality
Domestic, mindful, frugal. The app is about reducing food waste and managing household spending — it should feel considered and purposeful, not flashy.

### Aesthetic Direction
"Sustainable Kitchen Editorial" — forest green + warm cream + terracotta. Editorial typography (Gloock display serif + Epilogue grotesque body). Dark forest green hero and footer, warm cream content sections, terracotta accent for key numbers and CTAs. Feels like a quality food magazine redesigned as a digital product. Light theme overall with dark hero/footer bookends.

### Design Principles
1. **Editorial over decorative** — big type and generous whitespace do the work; no gratuitous decoration.
2. **Asymmetric grids** — the features section uses a 12-col grid with varied span sizes; avoid identical card grids.
3. **Stats as anchors** — food waste statistics (30–40%, €600) rendered in large terracotta serif type create emotional impact.
4. **Forest green = brand** — the deep green (`oklch(22% 0.065 155)`) is the primary brand color, used for the hero, active sprint, and footer; cream is the neutral canvas.
5. **Placeholders are first-class** — phone frames and team cards are built to be swapped with real content without design rework.
